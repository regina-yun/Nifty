How to add item to tracker:

If yes, kindly enter the full expiry date in the following format: dd/mm/yyyy and leave the Period-After-Opening as NA.
If no, kindly enter the date in which the product was opened in the following format: dd/mm/yyyy and select the appropriate POA value as per the item’s packaging label.
Once complete, click on ‘Add to list’ and the products’ name and expiry date will be calculated and generated on a list.

1. Expiration dates in cosmetics.
Most cosmetics do not have an exact expiry date printed on the packaging label. The expiry date depends on when the product was first opened. How long the product can be used depends on the “Period-After-Opening” or “PAO” for short. The PAO is a symbol with a numerical value on it. The numerical value refers to the shelf life in months. E.g., if the PAO states “12”, it will expire 12 months after opening.
2. What can cosmetics past its expiry date do to your skin?
Firstly, the most immediate that comes to your attention is that the colour of the product probably does not look as vibrant anymore, or it would not be as fresh, or it may smell a little different from what you are used to. Secondly, the moment the product is opened, it gets exposed to bacteria. If you continue to use it past its expiration date, you may risk getting an infection due to the accumulation of bacteria. Infections such as inflamed skin, rashes, blisters and even swelling may occur. Finally, certain chemical and ingredients in cosmetics may have started to break down once it is past their expiry date. Individuals with sensitive skin are prone to getting rashes and allergies.
